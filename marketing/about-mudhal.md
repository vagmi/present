# What is Present?

**Present is the solid foundation your software business gets built on — so you start from a real, working product instead of a blank page.**

*Present* (முதல்) is a Tamil word meaning "first," "beginning," and "capital" — the thing everything else is built on. That's the idea: get the foundation right, and the rest of your business has something dependable to stand on.

---

## The short version

If you want to build your own software — a booking system for your salon, a portal for your clients, a tool you'll sell to other businesses — you have two hard parts:

1. **The idea** — the thing that makes *your* product special. (You already have this.)
2. **The plumbing** — logins, accounts, teams, payments, security, keeping each customer's data private and separate. (Boring, invisible, and the same for almost every product on earth.)

Most people get stuck on the plumbing. **Present gives you the plumbing already built, tested, and ready** — so you spend your time on the idea.

---

## "I've tried Bolt / Lovable / ChatGPT — how is this different?"

Tools like Bolt are brilliant at one thing: turning a sentence into a **demo** in minutes. You type "build me a booking app" and something appears on screen. It feels like magic.

The catch shows up later. Those tools are great at the *front* of the app — the part you can see. They're much weaker at the *back* — the part you can't see but absolutely need the day you have real, paying customers:

| What a demo tool gives you | What a real business needs |
| --- | --- |
| A page that looks like an app | Customers who can sign up and log in securely |
| One pretend user | Real accounts, teams, and staff with different permissions |
| Fake data that resets | A real database that remembers everything, safely |
| "It works on my screen" | Keeping Customer A's data invisible to Customer B |
| A prototype to show a friend | A way to charge money and run free vs. paid plans |

Think of it like building a house. A demo tool hands you a beautiful **show home** — perfect for a photo, but there's no foundation, no wiring, no plumbing under the floor. The moment someone actually moves in, it falls apart.

**Present is the opposite.** It hands you the foundation, the wiring, and the plumbing first — the unglamorous parts that are genuinely hard to get right — and you build your rooms on top. It's less flashy in the first five minutes, and far more valuable in month six.

---

## What's already done for you

You don't need to understand any of these to use them — but here's what's in the box from day one:

- **🔐 Logins and accounts** — your customers can sign up, log in, reset passwords, and invite their teammates. Done properly and securely.
- **👥 Teams and roles** — businesses aren't one person. An owner can invite staff, and you decide who's allowed to do what (e.g. a manager can see billing, an assistant can't).
- **🛡️ Privacy between customers** — every customer's data is kept walled off from every other customer's, automatically. This is one of the easiest things to get dangerously wrong, and it's handled.
- **💳 Free and paid plans** — want a free tier and a £29/month tier? The ability to set limits ("free users get 5 of these, paid users get unlimited") is built in, and taking payments is one step away.
- **🗄️ A real, reliable database** — your data is stored safely and remembers everything, instead of vanishing when the page refreshes.
- **🎨 A clean, professional look** — it comes styled and presentable, not like a rough sketch.
- **✅ Quality checks built in** — the foundation is tested, so it behaves predictably as your product grows.

All of it runs on **Cloudflare's global network**, which means it's fast for your customers anywhere in the world and inexpensive to run.

## So how do *I* build my actual product?

You describe it in plain English to an AI coding assistant (Claude Code), and it builds your idea **on top of** the foundation Present provides.

The difference from starting with a blank AI chat: the AI isn't improvising the hard, risky parts from scratch every time. It's adding *your* features onto a structure that already does logins, teams, billing, and data privacy correctly. That's the difference between an assistant guessing and an assistant building on solid ground.

There's even a guided start — you answer a few questions about your idea ("who's it for? what does it do?") and it writes you a **step-by-step plan**, broken into small, shippable pieces, so you're never staring at a mountain.

## Who is Present for?

- **Local business owners** who want their own tool — a booking page, a client portal, a loyalty system — instead of paying monthly for someone else's, forever.
- **First-time SaaS founders** who have an idea to sell software to other businesses and want to launch something real, not just a prototype.
- **Anyone who's hit the wall** with a demo tool — loved the speed, then realised "wait, how do people actually log in and pay me?"

If you've ever thought *"I have the idea, I just don't know how to build the serious version,"* — that's exactly the gap Present fills.

---

## What Present is *not*

- It's **not** a no-code drag-and-drop builder. You (with an AI assistant) are building a genuine software product. It's more capable than no-code, and it asks a little more of you.
- It's **not** a finished app you just rename. It's the foundation and the example — your unique product is the part you add.
- It's **not** a monthly subscription that traps your work on someone else's platform. What you build is **yours**, running on your own account.
